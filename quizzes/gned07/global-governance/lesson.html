The Attributes of Today's Global System

  1. First, there are countries or states that are independent and govern themselves.
  2. Second, these countries interact with each other through diplomacy.
  3. Third, there are international organizations, like the United Nations (UN), that facilitate these interactions.
  4. Fourth, beyond simply facilitating meetings between states, international organizations also take on lives of their own.

Core Concepts

Diplomacy

Diplomacy is the established method of influencing the decisions and behavior of foreign governments and peoples through dialogue, negotiation, and other measures short of war or violence.

State

A state is a sovereign, organized political community with a centralized government, defined territory, permanent population, and the ultimate authority (sovereignty) to make and enforce laws, distinguished by its monopoly on the legitimate use of force.

A state has four elements

1) it exercises authority over a specific population, called citizens; 2) it governs a specific territory; 3) a state has a structure of government that crafts various rules that people (or the society) follow; and 4) most crucially, the state has sovereignty over its territory.

Sovereignty

Sovereignty is the supreme, independent authority a state holds over its own territory and people, meaning it has the ultimate power to govern itself without external interference.

Nation

A nation is a group of people who share a common language, history, culture, and usually a specific territory.

Benedict Anderson

According to Benedict Anderson, a nation is an "imagined community."

Nations usually limit membership to

People who share a culture, speak a common language, and live within a specific territory.

What Is Global Governance and What It Does

Global governance

Global governance simply means how countries work together to manage problems that go beyond national borders.

No single world government

There is no single "world government" that controls all countries.

How it emerged

Global governance emerged because many of today's problems go beyond the ability of one country to handle alone.

UN role

While there is no single central authority in global governance, the United Nations (UN) comes closest to this role.

UN can do

The UN can raise awareness of global issues, coordinate responses, and involve many non-state actors, including both governmental and non-governmental organizations.

UN began its operations on October 24, 1945

After World War II, the UN began its operations on October 24, 1945.

The Five Main UN Roles and Functions

1) Maintain International Peace and Security

The UN was created in 1945 after World War II to help prevent wars and conflicts around the world. It works by helping countries resolve disputes peacefully, sending peacekeepers to conflict areas, and creating conditions that allow peace to last.

2) Protect Human Rights

Promoting and protecting human rights is one of the UN's main goals. In 1948, the Universal Declaration of Human Rights was adopted, making human rights an international standard.

3) Deliver Humanitarian Aid

The UN helps countries when disasters or crises happen that they cannot handle alone. This includes natural disasters, wars, or other emergencies, providing food, shelter, medical care, and other support to people in need.

4) Promote Sustainable Development

Sustainable development means creating opportunities for economic growth, social well-being, and environmental protection. The UN works with countries to promote prosperity and a better quality of life for everyone.

5) Uphold International Law

The UN ensures that countries follow international laws and agreements through courts, treaties, and the Security Council, which can send peacekeepers, impose sanctions, or approve the use of force when necessary.

Six Key UN Organs

The General Assembly

The main discussion body of the UN. Every member country has equal representation.

The Security Council

Responsible for maintaining international peace and security. It has 15 members: five permanent members and ten non-permanent members elected for two-year terms.

The Economic and Social Council (ECOSOC)

Focuses on cooperation, policy review, and advice on economic, social, environmental, and related issues.

The Trusteeship Council

Created to oversee trust territories and ensure they moved toward independence and self-government. It suspended operations after the last trust territory became independent in 1994.

The International Court of Justice (ICJ)

The UN's main judicial body where countries can settle disputes through the law. It is in the Peace Palace in The Hague, Netherlands.

The Secretariat

Handles the day-to-day operations of the UN. It is led by the Secretary-General, appointed by the General Assembly on the recommendation of the Security Council.
